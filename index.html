About

From keeping pharma plants running to keeping AI agents running.

I spent fifteen years running maintenance for Pharmaceuticals industry and maintaining SAP ERP Business automation. Then I spent another three years as a SQL Server DBA — clustering, mirroring, performance tuning, the critical work of making sure nothing breaks at 3am. That background shapes how I build now.

When I started experimenting with n8n and voice agents, the appeal wasn't the novelty. It was recognizing the same problem I'd solved for two decades: systems that have to work when nobody's watching. A voice agent that drops a booking, a workflow that silently fails on webhook 40 out of 100 — that's a DBA's nightmare wearing an AI costume. So I build automations the way I built database infrastructure: with logging, escalation paths, and a healthy distrust of "it worked in testing."

Right now I'm full-time on the AI automation side — n8n and Make workflows, Retell AI voice agents, RAG pipelines on Supabase, and whatever integration a client's stack actually needs. If you want someone who'll obsess over the failure cases as much as the demo, that's me.
